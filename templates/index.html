Problem Statement

Educational institutions face the challenge of identifying students at risk of poor mathematics performance. Our regression model leverages socio-demographic and academic factors to predict math scores, enabling early intervention and personalized support strategies. By understanding the relationship between student background and academic achievement, educators can make data-driven decisions to improve learning outcomes.

🔬
Model Approach

We employ multiple regression techniques to analyze the complex relationships between student characteristics and mathematics performance. The model considers demographic factors, educational background, and academic preparation to generate accurate predictions. This comprehensive approach allows for nuanced understanding of factors influencing student success in mathematics.

📊
Dataset Overview & Feature Analysis

Our comprehensive student performance dataset captures diverse aspects of student demographics and academic background. Each feature provides valuable insights into factors that may influence mathematics achievement.

👤
Gender Distribution

Analysis of performance differences between male and female students, revealing potential gender-based learning patterns and achievement gaps in mathematics.

🌍
Race/Ethnicity Groups

Five distinct ethnic groups (A through E) providing insights into cultural and socioeconomic factors affecting mathematical achievement across diverse populations.

🎓
Parental Education Level

Ranging from "some high school" to "master's degree," this feature captures the significant impact of family educational background on student performance.

🍽️
Lunch Program Status

Students receiving standard vs. free/reduced lunch, serving as a proxy for socioeconomic status and its correlation with academic achievement.

📚
Test Preparation

Whether students completed test preparation courses, measuring the impact of additional academic support on mathematics performance.

📈
Multi-Subject Scores

Mathematics, reading, and writing scores enabling cross-subject correlation analysis and comprehensive academic performance assessment.
